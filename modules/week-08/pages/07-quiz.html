Quick quiz

Answer the questions below and then grade the quiz. You’ll get feedback for each question (why correct + why wrong). Nothing is saved.

1) Which statement best describes a project (as opposed to ongoing operations)?
A project is temporary and creates a unique deliverable (a map, model, dashboard, workflow, report, etc.).
Operations are ongoing and repetitive; software use doesn’t define a project; and projects can be led by different roles (not only someone with a PM title).
2) In a project charter, which item most clearly defines boundaries and prevents scope creep?
Clear scope boundaries (what you will and will not deliver) are the strongest guardrail against uncontrolled additions.
Colors and screenshots are presentation details, and tool preferences aren’t scope boundaries. A charter should make scope explicit.
3) In the classic triple constraint, which item is NOT one of the three corners?
The classic triple constraint is Scope, Time, and Cost/Resources (tradeoffs affect quality/value).
Stakeholder enthusiasm matters for project success, but it is not one of the classic three constraint corners.
4) Which is an example of a GIS “silent failure” risk?
Join/key issues can produce maps that look “fine” but are wrong. You often catch them only through validation.
The other choices are not data-integrity risks; they don’t silently invalidate an analysis.
5) A predictive (Waterfall) approach is usually best when…
Waterfall fits when you can plan sequential phases and change is limited.
Exploratory work and frequent demos align better with adaptive approaches. If the goal is unclear, you need initiation/scoping work first.
6) In an adaptive (Agile) approach, a “sprint” is…
Sprints are short iterations (often 1–2 weeks) that produce a reviewable increment and inform next priorities.
A kickoff is a meeting; risk lists are separate artifacts; and sprints happen throughout the project, not only at the end.
7) Which artifact is most focused on capturing technical steps, decisions, and “what we tried” for reproducibility?
A project log records technical steps and decisions so work can be reproduced, audited, and handed off.
A charter defines purpose/scope; Kanban shows status flow; stakeholder maps show relationships. None of those are step-by-step technical documentation.
8) In the hybrid lifecycle described in this module, which activity belongs in Monitoring & Control?
Monitoring & control keeps the project on track: status checks, blocker visibility, and adjustments based on progress and feedback.
Goals and success metrics belong in initiation; final handoff belongs in closure; and brainstorming topics is pre-initiation.
