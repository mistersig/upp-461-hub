Summary

Project management is how you turn GIS work into a reliable, reproducible deliverable.

Key takeaways

  • Projects are temporary and produce a unique deliverable.
  • PM aligns people, scope, time, resources, and risk to deliver value.
  • Waterfall fits stable requirements; Agile fits evolving work; Hybrid fits many GIS projects.
  • Artifacts matter: charter (why/what), plan (when), Kanban (status), log (how/reproducibility).

Common pitfalls

  • Unclear scope (“we’ll map everything”) → scope creep
  • No validation plan → silent failures
  • No documentation → work cannot be reproduced or handed off

Prep for class

  1. Draft a short project charter for your final project.
  2. Create a simple Kanban board with at least 6 tasks.
  3. Write 2 risks + 1 mitigation each.
