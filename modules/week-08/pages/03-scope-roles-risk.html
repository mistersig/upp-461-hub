Scope, roles, and risk

Most GIS project pain comes from unclear scope, unclear ownership, or untracked risks (especially the “silent failures”).

Core elements of a GIS project

  • Scope: what you will and will not deliver
  • Time: milestones and due dates
  • Cost / resources: people, compute, data, licenses
  • Quality: accuracy, completeness, reproducibility
  • Risk: what could go wrong (and how you’ll reduce it)

Typical roles

  • Project Manager (PM): scope, timeline, stakeholder communication
  • Data / ETL owner: sources, cleaning, joins, metadata
  • Analysis owner: methods, validation, sensitivity checks
  • Cartography / UX owner: readability + accessibility
  • QA / documentation owner: reproducibility + final packaging

Risk and “silent failures”

In GIS, some failures look like “the map is fine” until you validate.

  • Join keys mismatch (types, leading zeros, vintage)
  • Mixed coordinate reference systems
  • Unclear definitions (what counts as a “priority neighborhood”?)
  • Missing documentation / reproducibility

Practice: write down 2 risks for your final project and 1 mitigation for each.
