Tools: charter, plan, log, Kanban

Tools don’t replace thinking — they make coordination visible and protect reproducibility.

Project charter

Purpose: define the “why” and “what.”

  • Goal + scope (and what you will NOT do)
  • Stakeholders + decision-makers
  • Success metrics
  • Constraints and assumptions

Project plan

Milestones, deliverables, dependencies, deadlines.

Project log

Technical steps, decisions, “what we tried,” reproducibility.

Kanban board

Status visibility: To Do / In Progress / Waiting / Done.

Example Kanban

Mini activity

  1. List 6 tasks for your final project.
  2. Pick 2 that are blocked by dependencies (“Waiting”).
  3. Pick 1 you can do today (“In Progress”).

Tip: Keep tasks small enough to complete in 1–2 work sessions.
