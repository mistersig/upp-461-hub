Goal: make quantitative choropleth maps that are readable, defensible, and honest.

  • Pick a ramp that matches the data (sequential vs diverging).
  • Choose and justify class breaks using histograms.
  • Normalize when areas differ in population or size (rates/densities vs counts).

Estimated time: 20–35 minutes.

What you’ll do

  • Preview ramps (interactive)
  • Compare quantile / equal interval / Jenks breaks
  • Compute a rate for choropleth mapping

Rule of thumb

Try to limit choropleth classes to 5–7 colors unless you have a strong reason not to.
