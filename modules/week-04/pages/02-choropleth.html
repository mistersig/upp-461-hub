Choropleth basics

Thematic maps → choropleths

A choropleth map shades areas based on a numeric attribute (usually a rate or density).

  • Best for area units (tracts, counties, zones)
  • Best for ordered quantities (low → high)
  • Not ideal for raw totals when areas differ (normalize first)

Choropleth checklist

  • Normalization: percent, per capita, per area
  • Classification: how many classes and where breaks fall
  • Color: sequential vs diverging; enough contrast
  • Legend: readable classes; units clearly stated
