Normalization

Map rates, not counts

When areas vary in population or size, raw totals mostly map “size.” Normalize to a denominator:

  • Percent / proportion (part ÷ whole)
  • Per-capita rate (count ÷ population)
  • Density (count ÷ area)

Counts

Density (per area)

Try it: normalization calculator

Turn a raw count into a comparable rate (so your map is about intensity, not just “big places”).

Formula: rate = (count ÷ denominator) × per‑unit

Inputs

Example: number of crashes, crimes, calls, permits, or cases in each area.
Usually population (for a per‑capita rate) or area (for a density). Must be > 0.
Pick a standard scale that matches your topic (e.g., public health often uses per 100,000).

Output

Caption habit: Always include units in your legend/title (e.g., “crashes per 1,000 residents” or “cases per 100,000”).
