Data preparation & ETL

What is data preparation in GIS?

Data preparation is the work of cleaning, transforming, and formatting data so it’s ready for analysis, mapping, and integration with other datasets. In practice, this is often an ETL pipeline.

ETL: Extract → Transform → Load

  1. Extract: get data from its source (CSV, database, API, PDF table, etc.).
  2. Transform: clean + standardize (types, missing values, columns, geometry validity, projections, naming, etc.).
  3. Load: bring it into your GIS project / database in a consistent format.

Interoperability: why it matters

Interoperability is the ability for different systems and formats to work together: your GIS software, a database, a web map, and datasets from multiple organizations.

  • Data integration: join or combine sources without messy manual steps.
  • Efficiency: repeatable workflows beat “click until it works.”
  • Collaboration: shared standards make cross-team work possible.
Check yourself: Which ETL step is most likely to include cleaning column types and fixing missing values?

Answer: Transform.
