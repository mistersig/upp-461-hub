Summary

What you should be able to do

  • Describe ETL and identify what gets done during Transform.
  • Clean a CSV so it has consistent types, categories, and a reliable join key.
  • Choose Decennial Census vs ACS appropriately and interpret MOE.
  • Use TIGER boundary files + tabular tables together using GEOID.
  • Spot normalization problems (atomic values, primary key, functional dependence).

Common mistakes (avoid these)

  • Joining on a field that looks right but isn’t stable or unique.
  • Mixing geography levels (tract vs block group) or years/vintages.
  • Treating ACS estimates like exact counts (ignoring MOE).
