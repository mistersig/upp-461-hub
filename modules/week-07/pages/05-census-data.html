U.S. Census data

Decennial Census vs American Community Survey (ACS)

  • Decennial Census: full count (every 10 years).
  • ACS: sample survey → estimates for 1-year and 5-year periods, with Margin of Error (MOE).

Census geography: why it matters

Census variables are available at different geographic levels. Your choice of geography (tract, block group, etc.) affects what you can map and how reliable it is.

TIGER boundaries + tabular tables

The Census provides:

  • Tabular data (tables you download; often CSV)
  • Spatial boundaries (vector files, often from TIGER)
Check yourself: If you map ACS data, what should you do before making strong claims?

Answer: Check MOE and understand the variable definition + geography level. Small geographies can have large uncertainty.
