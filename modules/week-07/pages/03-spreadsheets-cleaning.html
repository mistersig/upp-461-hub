Spreadsheets & data cleaning

Common spreadsheet problems that break GIS

  • Mixed data types: numbers stored as text (e.g., “1,234” with commas).
  • Nulls & placeholders: blanks, “N/A”, “-999”, “Unknown” used inconsistently.
  • Non-atomic cells: multiple values in one cell (e.g., “A;B;C”).
  • Inconsistent categories: “IL”, “Illinois”, “Ill.” in the same field.
  • Bad identifiers: join key not unique, missing leading zeros, or changes meaning over time.

Cleaning moves (practical checklist)

  1. Freeze headers and confirm every column has one clear meaning.
  2. Normalize text: trim spaces, standardize case, standardize categories.
  3. Confirm types: IDs should be stored as text when leading zeros matter (common for GEOID).
  4. Find duplicates: your join key must be unique on at least one side of a join.
  5. Make cells atomic: split multi-value cells into separate rows or a related table.
Check yourself: Why might you store GEOID as text instead of a number?

Answer: Leading zeros matter. If GEOID is treated as a number, leading zeros can be dropped and joins will fail.
