Georeferencing

Georeferencing

To georeference means to associate something with locations in physical space.

In GIS, georeferencing often means assigning real-world coordinates to a scanned map or raster image so it aligns with other data.

Where georeferencing shows up in planning work

  • Historic maps and aerial photos (change over time)
  • Hand-drawn plans, sketches, or PDFs that need alignment
  • Digitizing features (roads, parcels, land use) from scanned sources

Try it (mental checklist)

  1. What’s my target coordinate system?
  2. Do I have good control points that are stable over time (intersections, bridges)?
  3. How many points do I need, and how well distributed are they?
  4. What accuracy is “good enough” for my use case?
Reminder: A “matched” image doesn’t mean it’s accurate—always evaluate residual error and fit for use.
