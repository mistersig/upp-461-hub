What is GIS?

Modern GIS is everywhere

So… what is GIS?

GIS (Geographic Information Systems) is a unique way of understanding and representing data in terms of its spatial properties.

Why GIS matters (because location matters)

  • Where are things happening?
  • What is happening there?
  • How do patterns relate to processes?

Planning examples: crime and poverty, health care access, food/water access, hazards, and environmental exposure.

How GIS is used

Communicative

Visualizing, displaying, and explaining patterns.

Analytical

Spatial analysis to solve problems and discover insights.

Try it: communication or analysis?

Pick the best fit for each example (some can be both—choose the dominant purpose).

Example: analysis + communication (CTA ridership)

GIS helps combine data and show inequities in patterns of mobility and access.

In practice, this kind of workflow blends data cleaning, spatial joins, and clear communication.
