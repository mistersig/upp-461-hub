Quick quiz

Quick quiz: Low-stakes self-check for Week 1.

Nothing is graded here; focus on the explanations.

1) Geography is primarily the study of…
Geography focuses on places and relationships in space (human + physical).
Geography is broader than countries or weather and includes spatial relationships.
2) Tobler’s Law is best summarized as…
It’s a rule of thumb: spatial proximity often implies stronger relationships.
The law emphasizes that distance usually matters for similarity and interaction.
3) GIS is best described as…
GIS connects data to place and supports mapping and spatial analysis.
GPS and paper maps are related tools, but GIS is broader and data-driven.
4) Which is an analytical use of GIS?
This is a spatial query (buffer/proximity analysis).
Formatting a map is communicative; analysis answers spatial questions.
5) To georeference means to…
Georeferencing ties an image to coordinates so it aligns with other spatial data.
Georeferencing is about location alignment, not styling or data model conversion.
6) ArcGIS Pro runs natively on…
ArcGIS Pro is Windows-only; macOS users typically use a Virtual Lab or virtualization.
macOS requires a workaround; mobile OSs are different app ecosystems.
7) Which filename best follows the course ArcGIS rules?
No spaces, no special characters, and begins with letters.
Spaces and special characters can break ArcGIS paths and tools.
8) Why is good data management important in GIS?
GIS projects often rely on file paths and linked datasets—organization prevents failures.
Good management helps workflow reliability, but doesn’t guarantee accuracy.
