Beyond the basics

This week focuses on the basics. But it helps to know what “advanced spatial analysis” usually means in GIS and planning research.

An overview of advanced techniques

Network analysis

  • Travel time / service areas
  • Routing (shortest path)
  • Accessibility metrics

Spatial interpolation

  • Estimate values between measurement points
  • Creates a surface (e.g., pollution, temperature)
  • Requires careful assumptions/validation

Hotspots / cluster analysis

  • Detect statistically significant clustering
  • Useful for targeting and prioritization

Spatial regression

  • Models outcomes with spatial dependence
  • Addresses “nearby places aren’t independent”
Check yourself: which technique fits?

Prompt: “What areas are within a 15-minute transit ride of a clinic?”

Best fit: network analysis (travel time / service area), not Euclidean buffers.

In-class exercise prep

  • Bring your questions about clip/dissolve/buffer and spatial joins.
