3) Projections and Projected Coordinate Systems (PCS)

A Projected Coordinate System (PCS) uses a mathematical map projection to turn curved Earth coordinates into a flat map. Values are typically in meters or feet.

Why project?

  • Distance/area calculations need linear units
  • Web maps often use a common projection for fast display
  • Local mapping needs low distortion in your region

Common projection surfaces

  • Cylindrical: good near the equator
  • Conic: good for mid-latitudes (east–west regions)
  • Planar/Azimuthal: good for polar regions
Key idea Every projection introduces distortion. Your job is to choose a projection that preserves what matters for your map.

Mini activity: degrees vs meters

Which is better for measuring a street length?

  • Geographic Coordinate System (GCS) in degrees (latitude/longitude)
  • Projected Coordinate System (PCS) in meters/feet
Show answer

Projected Coordinate System (PCS). Degrees are angular and not a constant distance across the Earth. A projected coordinate system uses linear units (meters/feet) so distance calculations make sense.
