2) Datum and Geographic Coordinate Systems (GCS)

If you remember only one thing: A datum and a projection are different jobs. Datums define the Earth model; projections flatten it.

Geographic Coordinate System (GCS)

A Geographic Coordinate System (GCS) uses latitude/longitude on a curved surface and typically reports values in degrees.

  • Latitude: -90 to 90
  • Longitude: -180 to 180
  • Units are angular (degrees), not distance

Datum

A datum describes how the Earth is modeled and aligned (ellipsoid + anchor). Different datums can place the “same” coordinate slightly differently on the ground.

Examples you may see: NAD 27, NAD 83, WGS 84.
