4) Distortion: what can a projection preserve?

When you flatten a globe, you can’t preserve everything at once. Projections typically preserve one property well, and distort others.

  • Conformal: preserves local shape/angles
  • Equal-area: preserves area (useful for choropleths)
  • Equidistant: preserves distance from a point/line (not everywhere)
  • Azimuthal: preserves direction from a point
Decision game If your map’s purpose is comparing sizes (e.g., land cover totals), prioritize equal-area. If your map needs accurate bearings or shapes (e.g., navigation-style), prioritize conformal.

Mini activity: Web Mercator

Web maps often use Web Mercator for display. What gets distorted most?

Area (especially near the poles). Regions look much larger than they really are.
